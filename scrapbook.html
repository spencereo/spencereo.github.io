We met outside a bar off Prospect Park

Graduating a semester early in December (before the pandemic hit,) I decided to pick up baking Sourdough as a new hobby to fill up the free time I was going to have as a Second Semeseter Senior. Thanks to my journalist cousin Morgan who asked New York Media Twitter for a sourdough starter, I got in touch with Cates, a writer at Buzzfeed who had been writing on sourdough-as-a-hobby as early as 2017.

Then, while I was back home for the holidays, I drove to Brooklyn - outside a bar right off of Prospect Park - waiting for Cates with her sourdough starter. She walked up to me with her friend and pulled out of her winter coat pocket a clear 4oz takeout container holding the Little Slimy that had just been separated from his colony, about to start his own, with me.

Like a drug deal there was no conversation. Thanks so much, I said with the takeout container holding the Little Slimy in my cold hands. And I'm suppose to feed this right? Yeah so you want to give it about a hundred grams of water and a hundred grams of flour. Technically you're suppose to do 125 water but I usually stick with a hundreed cuz it's just easier to remember. Also it's just white AP flour but you can add a bit of whole wheat if you want, that's why I do at least. And you want to feed it twice a day, disgarding the rest so you just have a hundred grams of starter when you do feed it. And don't worry I already fed this starter for you so you'll be good until tomorrow (I threw the tub into the fridge when I got back home and didn't touch it for weeks.)

Okay sounds good appreciate that. Cates and her friend headed into the bar. I put the takeout container with the Little Slimy into my winter jacket pocket and called my Mom to see if she could pick me up tonight from Prospect Park.
